DMLog.ai -- Session 4
Dungeon Master The tunnel opens into a vast natural cavern. Stalactites drip mineral-rich water into black pools that reflect no light. At the far end, hunched over a crude stone altar, you see a figure draped in tattered gray robes. It mutters in a language you cannot place -- though the cadence is unsettlingly familiar to Theron, given his time among the Exiled. The air smells of copper and burnt sage. A single torch gutters on the wall, casting long shadows that seem to move of their own accord. What do you do?
Theron (Rogue) I want to approach quietly, staying in the shadows along the left wall. Can I tell what the figure is doing at the altar? I am looking for any signs of a ritual or binding circle.
Dungeon Master Roll a Stealth check to approach unseen, and a Perception check to decipher the altar work.

Stealth (Theron): d20+7 = 19
Perception (Theron): d20+3 = 12

You move like smoke through the shadows. The figure does not notice you. From fifteen feet away, you can see it is arranging bones -- small ones, possibly from birds or rodents -- into a spiral pattern on the altar surface. This is a binding ward, but an imperfect one. Some of the bones are misplaced, which means either the figure is still learning, or it does not have all the components it needs. The muttering you hear is Old Exile cant, a dialect spoken only by those who spent time in the Eastern penal colonies. This person was imprisoned, like you were, Theron. You recognize the pattern of the ward. It is meant to contain something small. Something living.
Theron (Rogue) I step out of the shadows, hands open at my sides, and say in Old Exile cant: "You are building that ward wrong. The third ring needs thirteen bones, not eleven. Who are you trying to trap?"
