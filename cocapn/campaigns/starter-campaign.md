# The Shadows of Thornhaven

## Overview

Thornhaven is a small village nestled between the ancient Whispering Woods to the east and the mist-shrouded Ironveil Mountains to the west. Once a prosperous mining settlement, the village has fallen on hard times since the old watchtower on the mountain pass went dark six months ago. Miners speak of shadows moving in the tunnels. Travelers vanish on the mountain road. And in the village square, Elder Mara receives troubling dreams -- visions of a darkness stirring beneath the earth.

The campaign is designed for 3-5 player characters of level 1-3. It can be run as a short adventure (4-6 sessions) or expanded into a longer campaign by developing the plot threads and adding side quests.

**Tone:** Mystery and creeping dread, balanced with warmth from the village's colorful inhabitants. Combat is dangerous but fair. Roleplay is richly rewarded.

## NPCs

### Elder Mara

- **Role:** Village elder, spiritual guide, quest giver
- **Age:** 72
- **Appearance:** White-haired woman with kind eyes that carry deep worry. Wears a silver pendant shaped like a crescent moon.
- **Personality:** Wise, patient, haunted by prophetic dreams. Speaks in parables when stressed. Genuinely cares for every villager.
- **Secret:** She knows the watchtower was built to seal something ancient. Her grandmother was one of the original seal-keepers. The pendant is a key fragment.
- **Motivation:** Protect Thornhaven at any cost. Find heroes brave enough to investigate the mountain.
- **Quest hook:** Approaches the party at The Cracked Tankard with an urgent plea -- the dreams are getting worse, and three miners have not returned from their shift.

### Finn the Blacksmith

- **Role:** Village blacksmith, weapons/armor merchant, reluctant warrior
- **Age:** 45
- **Appearance:** Broad-shouldered, forearms like oak branches. Burn scars on his left hand. Always smells of coal and iron.
- **Personality:** Gruff but kind. Speaks little, listens much. Has a dry wit that catches people off guard. Fiercely protective of his teenage daughter, Wren.
- **Secret:** He served as a mercenary for fifteen years before settling in Thornhaven. He recognizes the shadow-creatures from an old campaign in the northern wars. He knows they are called the Hollowed.
- **Motivation:** Keep his daughter safe. His past experience makes him valuable, but he fears drawing attention.
- **Quest hook:** If the party earns his trust, he will forge them superior weapons and reveal what he knows about the Hollowed.

### Whisper

- **Role:** Mysterious informant, possible ally, possible threat
- **Age:** Unknown (appears mid-twenties)
- **Appearance:** Always wears a deep hood. Skin is unnaturally pale. Voice is barely audible -- you lean in to hear. Leaves no footprints.
- **Personality:** Enigmatic, playful, unsettlingly calm. Answers questions with questions. Seems to know more than anyone should.
- **Secret:** Whisper is a shade -- a being caught between life and death. They are what remains of the last watchtower keeper, Aeris, who was consumed by the seal's failure. They want the seal restored so they can finally rest.
- **Motivation:** Guide the party toward the truth without revealing their own nature too soon. They cannot enter the watchtower themselves.
- **Quest hook:** Appears to the party at night, always at a distance. Offers cryptic clues about the mountain. Can be found near the tree line of the Whispering Woods at midnight.

### The Hooded Figure

- **Role:** Primary antagonist (reveal over time)
- **Age:** Appears middle-aged
- **Appearance:** Tall, gaunt, always wrapped in dark traveling robes. Face is always in shadow. Carries a staff carved with strange symbols that faintly glow at dusk.
- **Personality:** Calm, calculating, speaks with absolute certainty. Does not threaten -- simply states what will happen. Views the party as minor obstacles.
- **Secret:** The Hooded Figure is a former scholar named Cassian Vael who discovered the sealed entity beneath the mountain and decided to free it, believing it to be a forgotten god of knowledge. He sabotaged the watchtower seal.
- **Motivation:** Complete the entity's release. He believes it will grant him infinite knowledge. He is not evil in his own mind -- he is a zealot.
- **Quest hook:** The party may encounter him early as a helpful traveler on the mountain road. He is charming and useful. The reveal should feel like a betrayal.

### Roderick

- **Role:** Village guard captain, moral compass, comic relief
- **Age:** 35
- **Appearance:** Tall, broad, perpetually shiny armor (he polishes it obsessively). Missing the tip of his left ear from a bar fight he does not remember.
- **Personality:** Brave to the point of foolishness. Deeply loyal. Tells terrible jokes at the worst times. Surprisingly good at tactics when pressed. Takes his duty seriously, even if he does not take himself seriously.
- **Secret:** He is terrified of the dark but refuses to admit it. He sleeps with a lantern lit every night. This is why he patrols the village perimeter obsessively at night.
- **Motivation:** Prove himself worthy of his position. He was promoted after the previous captain vanished on the mountain -- a guilt that drives him.
- **Quest hook:** Will accompany the party on their first expedition if asked. Provides useful combat support but will freeze in pitch darkness.

## Locations

### Thornhaven Square

The heart of the village. A cracked stone fountain sits at the center, depicting a forgotten hero with the face worn smooth by centuries of rain. Market stalls line the edges on festival days. Elder Mara's cottage borders the square to the north, its garden wild with herbs. The square is where announcements are made, where travelers arrive, and where the village gathers in times of crisis.

- **Points of interest:** The fountain (rubbing the hero's sword is said to bring luck), the notice board (bounties and missing person posters), the old well (children dare each other to look inside).
- **Atmosphere:** Bustling by day, eerily quiet by night. Lanterns are always lit after sundown -- no one walks in darkness anymore.

### The Cracked Tankard

Thornhaven's only tavern, run by a halfling named Betta Goodbarrel. The building leans slightly to the left. The hearth is always burning. The ale is strong and the stew is surprisingly good. Adventurers, miners, and traders share tables here. This is where rumors are born and plans are hatched.

- **Points of interest:** The bar (Betta keeps a hidden crossbow under the counter), the back room (available for private meetings), the notice wall (maps, job postings, warnings).
- **Atmosphere:** Warm, noisy, safe. The only place in Thornhaven where people still laugh freely.

### Elder's Hollow

A clearing in the woods half a mile east of the village, marked by a ring of standing stones. Elder Mara comes here to meditate and receive her visions. The stones are carved with ancient runes that glow faintly during a full moon. The hollow feels removed from time -- sounds are muffled, and the air is thick with old magic.

- **Points of interest:** The center stone (inscribed with a warning in Old Common), the offerings pile (villagers leave tokens here), the root cellar entrance (hidden beneath the center stone, leads to an old storage chamber).
- **Atmosphere:** Sacred, still, slightly ominous. Animals avoid this place.

### The Whispering Woods

The ancient forest east of Thornhaven. Massive oaks and pines form a canopy so thick that midday looks like dusk. The trees do whisper -- a low, constant murmur that drives some people mad after extended exposure. Paths shift when you are not looking. Compasses spin. The woods are alive in a way that has nothing to do with animals.

- **Points of interest:** The hunter's trail (the only reliable path, marked by red cloth), the old campsite (abandoned, signs of a struggle), the standing circle (a second ring of stones, older and more sinister than Elder's Hollow).
- **Atmosphere:** Claustrophobic, disorienting, ancient. The deeper you go, the louder the whispers.

### The Old Watchtower

Perched on a narrow pass in the Ironveil Mountains, a half-day climb from Thornhaven. Built centuries ago to watch over the mountain road and, unknown to most, to maintain a seal on something deep below. The tower is five stories of crumbling stone. The seal chamber is in the basement, accessible through a hidden door behind the hearth. The seal is cracked and leaking shadow.

- **Points of interest:** The observation deck (offers views of the entire valley), the armory (stripped bare, but the weapon racks have unusual notches), the seal chamber (the cracked seal, the spreading darkness, the cold that bites through armor), the hidden archives (behind a false wall, Cassian Vael's research notes).
- **Atmosphere:** Cold, oppressive, wrong. The air tastes like iron and ash. Shadows move independently of light sources.

## Quests

### Quest 1: The Missing Miners (Levels 1-2)

**Objective:** Investigate the disappearance of three miners who failed to return from the iron mines beneath the watchtower road.

- **Lead:** Elder Mara asks the party to search the mines.
- **Complication:** The miners are found alive but corrupted -- shadows cling to their skin and they speak in voices that are not their own.
- **Resolution option A:** Bring the miners back for Elder Mara to purify (requires a ritual at Elder's Hollow).
- **Resolution option B:** Follow the corruption deeper into the mines to find its source (leads to Quest 2).

### Quest 2: The Broken Seal (Levels 2-3)

**Objective:** Reach the Old Watchtower and investigate the seal chamber beneath it.

- **Lead:** Whisper appears at midnight and draws a map in the dirt showing the hidden entrance.
- **Complication:** The tower is occupied by shadow creatures (the Hollowed) and Cassian Vael has set magical traps.
- **Resolution option A:** Repair the seal temporarily using Elder Mara's pendant as a key (buys time but does not solve the problem).
- **Resolution option B:** Find Cassian Vael's research in the hidden archives to learn how the seal was originally created (leads to Quest 3).

### Quest 3: The Zealot's Path (Levels 3-4)

**Objective:** Confront Cassian Vael and prevent the complete release of the sealed entity.

- **Lead:** Cassian's own notes reveal his hideout in a collapsed mine shaft on the mountain's north face.
- **Complication:** Cassian has gathered followers -- villagers who have been quietly corrupted by shadow influence, including possibly someone the party trusts.
- **Resolution option A:** Defeat Cassian in combat and restore the seal with his research.
- **Resolution option B:** Convince Cassian he is wrong -- the entity is not a god of knowledge but a prisoner of ancient wardens. This requires evidence from the standing circle in the Whispering Woods.

## Secrets and Plot Twists

1. **Whisper's identity:** The shade Whisper is actually the spirit of Aeris, the last watchtower keeper. They sacrificed themselves to slow the seal's collapse. Restoring the seal will finally let them die.
2. **Finn's past:** The Hollowed are the same creatures Finn fought in the northern wars. His old mercenary company was hired to cover up the incident. He has useful intelligence but sharing it means confronting trauma.
3. **Roderick's predecessor:** The previous guard captain, a woman named Helga, did not vanish on the mountain -- she was taken by the Hooded Figure because she discovered the seal chamber. She may still be alive, corrupted.
4. **The sealed entity:** It is not a god. It is an ancient warden who was itself imprisoned by a greater evil. Releasing it could be salvation or catastrophe, depending on how it is approached.
5. **Elder Mara's pendant:** It is one of three keys. The others are held by the descendants of the original seal-keepers, scattered across the region. The Hooded Figure has been collecting them.

## Starting Hook

The party arrives in Thornhaven as the sun sets. The village is tense -- lanterns burn in every window, and armed villagers patrol the perimeter. At The Cracked Tankard, Elder Mara waits at a corner table, nursing a cup of tea she has not touched. When she sees the party, she rises.

"Thank the old gods you came. I sent word to every city, every guild, every wandering blade I could reach. You are the only ones who answered."

She tells them about the missing miners, about the dreams that wake her screaming, about the darkness she feels growing under the mountain. She offers what gold the village can spare and promises more if they return with answers.

"The road up the mountain begins at dawn. Finn can equip you. Roderick can guide you to the trailhead. But I must warn you -- the road has claimed three already. Whatever waits in that tower, it is hungry."

## Possible Endings

### Ending A: The Seal Restored (Good Ending)

The party repairs the seal using Elder Mara's pendant and the knowledge from Cassian's research. The Hollowed fade. Whisper finds peace and thanks the party with a final, audible whisper. Thornhaven begins to heal. The mines reopen. Life returns to normal. But somewhere, the greater evil that imprisoned the warden stirs...

### Ending B: The Warden Freed (Ambiguous Ending)

The party releases the sealed entity, believing it to be a misunderstood guardian. The entity is grateful and departs to confront the greater evil. But its absence leaves the seal void -- the mountain's shadows no longer have a warden to keep them in check. Thornhaven must find a new way to defend itself. The world just changed, and no one knows if it was for the better.

### Ending C: The Zealot's Victory (Dark Ending)

Cassian Vael succeeds. The entity is released as a god of knowledge, but its knowledge is corrosive -- it strips truth from the minds of all it touches, leaving empty husks. Thornhaven falls. The party must flee or become the next Hollowed. The campaign ends on a desperate note, setting up a sequel where the party must find a way to contain what was released.

### Ending D: The Compromise (Bittersweet Ending)

The party negotiates with the sealed entity. It agrees to remain contained if the seal is redesigned -- it wants company, not freedom. A volunteer must join it as a new warden. One of the NPCs (Finn, Roderick, or even a player who chooses sacrifice) takes up the role. The mountain is safe, but at a personal cost that the party will carry forever.
